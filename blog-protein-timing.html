Does Protein Timing Actually Matter?

Published Jul 3, 2026·5 min read

On this page

The "anabolic window" myth What actually matters more Practical guidance

For years, gym culture treated the 30-60 minutes after a workout as a narrow window that had to be hit with protein or the workout's benefits would be wasted. The research tells a more relaxed story.

The "anabolic window" myth

Meta-analyses that isolate timing from total daily protein intake generally find the timing effect is small to negligible once total daily protein is controlled for. Hitting your daily target matters far more than hitting a specific clock time.

What actually matters more

Total daily protein intake, consistent training, and adequate sleep outperform precise timing in the research record. Distributing protein across 3-5 meals appears to help slightly compared to eating it all in one sitting.

Practical guidance

Eat a protein-containing meal sometime within a few hours of training, hit your daily gram target consistently, and don't stress about the stopwatch. Consistency across weeks and months is what drives results.
