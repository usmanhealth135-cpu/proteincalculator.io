The Fundamentals

Why protein matters more than most people think

Protein is the only macronutrient that directly supplies the amino acids your body uses to build and repair tissue — it can't be substituted by carbs or fat.

Muscle repair & growth

Amino acids from protein trigger muscle protein synthesis, the process that repairs training damage and builds new lean tissue.

Satiety & appetite control

Gram for gram, protein is the most filling macronutrient, which is why higher-protein diets tend to reduce spontaneous overeating.

Preserves lean mass in a deficit

Adequate protein during weight loss protects muscle tissue, so more of the weight lost comes from fat rather than lean mass.
